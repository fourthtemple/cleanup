Model Cleanup Recipes

Quick Start

  1. Enter a folder name for the character or project.
  2. Click Create Folder.
  3. Click Import To Folder and choose the FBX, GLB, or GLTF files.
  4. Click a library item to load it, or click its delete button if you do not want it.
  5. Make edits, then use Save or Save As to keep the cleanup in browser storage.

Painting And Selection

  1. Use Pen for 2D raycast projection painting, or Neighbor to avoid accidental selections by mapping to the initial vertex neighborhood.
  2. Adjust Selection radius; enable Through when you want the brush to pass through the mesh.
  3. Use Airbrush for color work, or Clear Selection, Reset All, and Invert Selection to reshape the selected region.

FK/IK Posing And Keys

  1. Select Shoulder, Arm, Forearm, and Hand, click Add Chain, then choose the chain and hand.
  2. Use FK with Rotate or Move for direct posing.
  3. Use IK to drag a limb target.
  4. Press Key.
  5. Open the timeline curve editor for greater control.

Bone Chains And Distribution

  1. Open Rig, select ordered items in Chain bones, then click Add Chain.
  2. The chain is already assigned; go back and make a selection only if you need to adjust the region.
  3. Press Distribute Chain to spread the selected vertices across the chain.

Joint Constraints

  1. Select the joint you want to limit and turn Joint Constraints On.
  2. Pose the joint at its lower limit and click Min Pose.
  3. Pose the joint at its upper limit and click Max Pose; use Clear to remove the limit.

Root Motion Cleanup

  1. Load an animation where the hips carry travel motion.
  2. Click Unbake Root to separate root travel from hip posing.
  3. Preview the result, then Save before exporting for Unreal or Unity workflows.

Export

  1. Choose an FBX Target such as Three.js/Web, Blender, Unity, Unreal, or Maya.
  2. Use Export FBX for rigged animation pipelines.
  3. Use Export GLB when you want a web-friendly scene export.
